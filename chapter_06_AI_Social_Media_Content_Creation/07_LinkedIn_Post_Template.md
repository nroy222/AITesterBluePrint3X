# 07 — LinkedIn Post Template (+ How to Publish for Reach)

A LinkedIn post that earns the "…see more" click and sparks comments — plus the publishing
mechanics that decide whether 200 or 20,000 people see it. Pull from
[00](00_Hook_Story_Offer_Planning.md).

> LinkedIn's algorithm rewards two things above all: the **dwell** your first two lines buy, and
> the **comments** your last line provokes. Everything here serves those two.

---

## The post

### Format rules
- **First 2 lines must earn the "see more"** — curiosity gap, alarming truth, or a question the
  reader is already half-thinking. Everything after the fold is invisible until they click.
- **Use → arrows for lists. Never bullet points. Never numbered lists.** (Bullets read as
  corporate; arrows read as human.)
- **Length:** under 280 words (sweet spot 220–260). For a "Justin Welsh cut": 140–200 words,
  single-line paragraphs, heavy whitespace.
- **1–2 screenshot-worthy lines** — standalone, quotable.
- **End with a specific PS question** — binary or specific, never "what do you think?" Closers
  that work: "Honest answers only." / "Real numbers only." / "No judgment."
- **5–7 hashtags** at the end.
- **No emojis** (1–2 strategic, almost never).

### Hook patterns
- "It is happening in [field] right now."
- "Unpopular opinion."
- A question they already think ("AI assistants get dumber after a few hours. Noticed?")
- An interview/story cold-open ("A candidate came to my interview yesterday…")
- A blunt reversal ("Your 5,000 tests are not an asset. They're a liability.")

### Fill-in template
```
LINE 1–2 (HOOK — earn the "see more"):
___________________________________________________
___________________________________________________

(whitespace)

BODY (→ arrows for any list, single-line paragraphs, lots of whitespace):
→ ________________________________________________
→ ________________________________________________
→ ________________________________________________

SCREENSHOT LINE (quotable, standalone):
___________________________________________________

PS QUESTION (specific/binary — never "what do you think?"):
PS: _______________________________________________

HASHTAGS (5–7): ____________________________________
```

## How to actually publish it (this is half the result)

1. **No links in the post body.** LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound body links.
   Put the article/video/repo link in the **first comment** instead — post the comment yourself
   immediately after publishing.
   > Exception: when you're deliberately promoting a product, a soft mentor-framed CTA at the very
   > end is fine — just know you're trading some reach for the click.
2. **Post when your audience is active** — test mornings (your timezone) on weekdays. Consistency
   beats any single "perfect time."
3. **The first 60–90 minutes decide everything.** Reply to every comment fast — each reply is a
   signal that lifts the post. Ask a follow-up question back to keep the thread alive.
4. **Don't edit in the first hour** if you can avoid it (edits can dampen distribution). Proofread
   before posting.
5. **Native > everything.** Type the post in LinkedIn, not pasted with weird formatting. For
   images, a clean tweet-screenshot style (black bg, white text, realistic varied metrics) is the
   highest-performing visual — no logo, no watermark, no decoration; the minimalism is the design.
6. **Repurpose, don't cross-post raw.** Adapt the same Hook · Story · Offer for
   [Instagram](03_Instagram_Post_Template.md) and [Medium](05_Medium_Article_Template.md) rather
   than pasting the identical text everywhere.

## Pre-publish checklist
- [ ] First 2 lines earn the "see more" (and it's not a stat)
- [ ] → arrows, no bullets, no numbered lists
- [ ] Under 280 words (or a clean 140–200 "Welsh cut")
- [ ] 1–2 screenshot-worthy standalone lines
- [ ] Specific/binary PS question — not "what do you think?"
- [ ] 5–7 hashtags
- [ ] No link in the body — link queued for the first comment
- [ ] Ready to reply to every comment in the first 60–90 minutes
- [ ] No banned phrases, no fabricated numbers
